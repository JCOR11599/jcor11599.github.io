Sudoku Solver

Constraint-Solving to Solve Sudoku Puzzles

A popular Japanese puzzle game, Sudoku, is a simple, yet complex game. The whole goal is to fill all the squares with the numbers 1-9 such that there are no repeated numbers in each row, column, or subsquare.

Of course we can't just guess every permutation of a sudoku grid until we get a solution that's correct. There are 81 squares in total (9 x 9) and 9 possibilities for each square (numbers 1-9). In the worst possible case, we have 9^81 possible grids and that's quite a bit.

Luckily, some squares are already filled with numbers, so we can use our constraints (no number can repeat in a row, column, and subsquare), to grealty reduce the number of possibiblities. And that's exactly what our AI does, it eliminates solutions it knows is incorrect until a square only has only 1 possible solution. From here, the AI can further constrain the problem. However, at some point, there are no squares left with only 1 possible choice. So, we're forced to guess; we just have to keep track of our guesses and pick the other choice when we find out that this guess can't lead to a correct solution.
